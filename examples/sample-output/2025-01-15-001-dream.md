# Muse — 2025-01-15 — 001
Today's leverage is turning Sam's repeated audit pattern into a calmer, more predictable consulting system.

## Seeds consumed

- A prospect on a call asked "do you offer retainers?" — Sam said "not yet".
- Saw a newsletter issue about "productized consulting" and bookmarked it.

## Recurring threads

- The repeatable audit shape keeps resurfacing: intake call, read-only access,
  current-state map, top friction points, quick wins, and 90-day roadmap.
- The ledger says not to pursue a SaaS replacement, so this dream keeps the
  leverage in service packaging and workflow, not software product development.

## Insights

1. Sam is already doing productized work, but selling it like bespoke hourly work.
   The hidden asset is the repeated report structure.
2. Prospects are asking for continuity after audits. The retainer question is not
   a distraction; it is demand signal.
3. Energy protection is a business design constraint, not a personal afterthought.
   A better offer should reduce discovery-call load.

## Ideas

### 1. Package the audit as a fixed-scope starter offer
**Idea ID:** muse-20250115-001-a
**Lens:** offers / productization
**What it is:** Turn the recurring audit deliverable into a named starter offer.
Instead of scoping every engagement from scratch, Sam sells a bounded audit with a
known intake, artifact, timeline, and decision point.
**Why now:** The last three audits had the same shape, and a prospect just asked
about a more continuous relationship.
**How:** Extract the repeated report sections, write inclusion/exclusion rules,
name the offer, and test it with the next qualified referral.
**First step:** List the sections that were identical across the last three audit reports.
**Effort / impact:** low / high
**Risks:** The package could overfit one client type; mark it as version 1.
**Assumption:** Assumes the three audit reports are structurally similar because the audit notes say they shared the same sections.
**Recombined nodes:** `memory/wiki/clients/audit-notes.md` × `memory/wiki/offers/index.md` × `dreams/seeds.md` retainer seed.
**Confidence:** medium — the pattern is visible in notes, but the actual reports have not been diffed.
**Falsifier:** If the last three reports differ substantially, there is no stable package to extract.
**Connects to:** `memory/wiki/offers/index.md`
**Sources:** None; internal sample vault only.
**Route:** build

### 2. Offer a 30-day implementation retainer after each audit
**Idea ID:** muse-20250115-001-b
**Lens:** current clients/customers
**What it is:** Add a small, time-boxed implementation retainer as the default
next step after the audit. It should help clients act on quick wins without
turning Sam's calendar into unlimited hourly support.
**Why now:** Audit notes say clients keep asking for implementation help, and the
seed says a prospect asked about retainers.
**How:** Define one retainer tier, cap communication windows, pick three eligible
quick-win categories, and attach it as an optional next step in the audit report.
**First step:** Draft the retainer boundaries: duration, response window, included work, and explicit exclusions.
**Effort / impact:** medium / high
**Risks:** If boundaries are loose, the retainer recreates the burnout problem.
**Assumption:** Assumes clients want help implementing the roadmap, based on repeated post-audit asks in the sample notes.
**Recombined nodes:** `memory/wiki/clients/audit-notes.md` × `dreams/seeds.md` retainer seed × `memory/wiki/goals.md` energy protection goal.
**Confidence:** medium — demand appears repeated, but willingness to pay for a bounded retainer is untested.
**Falsifier:** If the next two audit clients decline a bounded retainer, demand may be conversational rather than commercial.
**Connects to:** `memory/wiki/goals.md`, `memory/wiki/offers/index.md`
**Sources:** None; internal sample vault only.
**Route:** decision

### 3. Create a weekly no-call audit production block
**Idea ID:** muse-20250115-001-c
**Lens:** health & performance
**What it is:** Protect one weekly block for audit production and learning. The
business change only works if the calendar stops absorbing every discovery call.
**Why now:** Sam's goals include protecting energy, and the overview says discovery
calls are filling the calendar.
**How:** Pick one recurring block, mark it no-call, and use it for report template
refinement plus one learning input that sharpens the offer.
**First step:** Block a recurring 2-hour no-call window before accepting more discovery calls.
**Effort / impact:** low / medium
**Risks:** If referrals are the only pipeline, saying no to calls may feel risky.
**Assumption:** Assumes Sam has enough calendar control to protect one block per week, inferred from solo work context.
**Recombined nodes:** `memory/wiki/overview.md` × `memory/wiki/goals.md` protect energy goal × recurring audit template theme.
**Confidence:** high — the constraint is explicit and the action is reversible.
**Falsifier:** If the calendar is externally controlled and cannot be blocked, this needs a different boundary mechanism.
**Connects to:** `memory/wiki/goals.md`, `memory/wiki/overview.md`
**Sources:** None; internal sample vault only.
**Route:** task
